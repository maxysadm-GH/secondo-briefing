Anticipated questions — and our answers

"Isn't this just a ChatGPT wrapper with extra steps?" No. The patent claim is an AI agent that joins outbound phone calls on behalf of the user, with live context-aware retrieval from an encrypted personal vault. OpenAI doesn't do this. Pi doesn't do this. Rabbit and Humane tried the hardware version and failed. We ship the software version on iOS, voice-first, with documents the user already has. "HIPAA?" Secondo is a general-purpose personal assistant — scheduling, documents, calls. Not a healthcare product. Navilum is the HIPAA-adjacent sibling and is deliberately separated. Clean regulatory story. "Why will incumbents (Apple Intelligence, Google Gemini, Siri) not just ship this?" Provisional patent on the call-agent-with-vault claim. Incumbents are constrained by: (a) first-party OS assistants can't originate calls on the user's behalf without elaborate carrier deals, (b) their privacy architecture won't let them cross the vault→call boundary, (c) we ship the vault as an encrypted user-owned artifact, not a cloud index. "What's traction?" Internal alpha only. Founder as the primary user. We are deliberately not quoting fabricated user counts. Patent demo Apr 28; TestFlight invite-out begins after Track-A P0 fixes ship. "Why a SAFE and not priced?" Per Ned (corporate counsel, 4/17): Chicago norms support $500K–$1M SAFE → $1.5–3M priced seed. Keeps cap table clean, defers valuation pressure until we have working voice-to-call demo video + provisional patent filed. "What kills this?" Apple Intelligence shipping a native vault-to-call feature in iOS 19. Mitigation: file provisional first (Apr 30), build enterprise compilation-layer moat (Patent #2, Jun 30), and win consumer on a non-Apple-aligned wedge (power users with 40+ accounts who want an encrypted vault Apple can't see into).
