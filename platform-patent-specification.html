I Abstract

A system and method for a voice-activated artificial intelligence assistant that operates primarily on-device for privacy, maintains a persistent encrypted document vault accessible by natural language voice queries, and autonomously participates in telephone communications on behalf of the user with real-time access to the user's stored documents. The system comprises: (1) an on-device wake word detection module that activates without cloud connectivity; (2) an on-device speech-to-text engine that processes voice commands locally without transmitting audio data; (3) an on-device optical character recognition module that captures, classifies, and indexes physical documents through a voice-guided categorization wizard; (4) an encrypted local-first document vault organized in user-defined hierarchical categories with vector-indexed natural language search; (5) a voice response synthesizer that reads stored document content aloud in response to natural language queries; (6) an autonomous call management module that initiates outbound telephone calls, navigates automated phone systems (IVR), and maintains the AI as a persistent participant during live calls with real-time access to the document vault; and (7) a post-call intelligence module that extracts action items, dates, and commitments from call transcriptions and automatically generates calendar events and reminders. The platform is designed as a modular, application-agnostic engine that powers multiple domain-specific applications through a common application interface layer, enabling rapid deployment across vertical markets including healthcare coordination and personal life management.

II Technical Field

The present invention relates generally to voice-activated computing systems and, more particularly, to systems and methods for voice-activated artificial intelligence assistants that combine on-device natural language processing, encrypted document management, autonomous telecommunications capabilities, and on-device machine learning. The invention further relates to modular platform architectures that enable a single core engine to power multiple domain-specific applications with specialized workflows and compliance requirements.

III Background of the Invention

Field Context and Problem Statement

Voice-activated digital assistants have become commonplace in consumer electronics, with systems such as Apple Siri, Amazon Alexa, and Google Assistant deployed on billions of devices worldwide. However, these existing systems suffer from several critical limitations that prevent them from serving as comprehensive personal information management and communication tools.

Limitations of Existing Voice Assistants

Cloud Dependency and Privacy Concerns. Current voice assistants transmit substantially all spoken audio to remote cloud servers for processing. This architectural design creates significant privacy concerns, particularly for sensitive personal information such as medical records, financial documents, legal agreements, and identity credentials. Users who wish to manage sensitive life information through voice interfaces are forced to accept the transmission of that information to third-party servers, often with opaque data retention and usage policies. This cloud-dependent architecture also renders these systems inoperable during network outages, precisely when users may most urgently need access to critical personal information.

Absence of Persistent Document Context. No existing commercially available voice assistant maintains a persistent, encrypted, user-controlled document vault that can be queried through natural language voice commands. While cloud-based document management platforms (e.g., Notion, Evernote, Google Drive, Apple Notes) provide text-based document storage and search, these systems are fundamentally text-first interfaces with voice capabilities added as a secondary interaction modality. They do not support voice-guided document capture, voice-first categorization workflows, or the ability to speak a natural language question and receive a synthesized voice response that reads relevant information from stored documents.

No Autonomous Call Participation. Perhaps most significantly, no existing system combines document awareness with autonomous telephone communication capabilities. Users managing complex life situations—chronic illness, elder care, legal disputes, insurance claims, financial planning—frequently spend substantial time on telephone calls with service providers, during which they must manually locate and reference relevant documents. These calls often involve extended hold times, navigation of complex automated phone systems (IVR trees), and the need to provide information (account numbers, policy numbers, identification numbers, dates) that is stored in physical or digital documents. No existing system can autonomously initiate such calls, navigate IVR systems using stored information, wait on hold on behalf of the user, participate in live calls with real-time document access, or extract actionable intelligence from completed calls.

Unmet Need

There exists a significant unmet need for a voice-first AI assistant system that: (a) processes voice data primarily on-device to protect user privacy; (b) maintains a persistent, encrypted, hierarchically organized document vault accessible through natural language voice queries; (c) can autonomously initiate and participate in telephone communications with real-time access to stored documents; and (d) extracts actionable intelligence from completed calls to automate follow-up tasks. Furthermore, there is a need for such a system to be architected as a modular platform capable of powering multiple domain-specific applications—such as healthcare coordination and personal life management—through a common core engine with application-specific extension modules.

IV Summary of the Invention

The present invention provides a voice-activated artificial intelligence assistant system and platform that addresses the foregoing limitations and unmet needs. In preferred embodiments, the system comprises a mobile computing device executing an application that integrates the following interconnected modules:

  1. On-Device Wake Word Detection Module — A continuously-running, low-power wake word detection engine that operates entirely on-device without network connectivity, consuming less than approximately 5% of device battery capacity, and that activates the speech processing pipeline upon detection of one or more configurable wake word phrases.
  2. On-Device Speech-to-Text Engine — A local inference engine that converts spoken audio to text entirely on the mobile device without transmitting audio data to any remote server, supporting real-time transcription, batch processing, voice activity detection, and multiple languages.
  3. On-Device Optical Character Recognition Module with Voice-Guided Categorization — A module that captures images of physical documents through the device camera, extracts text content using on-device OCR, and engages the user in a voice-guided categorization wizard to classify and organize captured documents within the hierarchical vault.
  4. Encrypted Hierarchical Document Vault — A local-first encrypted storage system organized in user-defined hierarchical categories, wherein each document is stored with its raw image, extracted text, structured fields, vector embedding, and metadata, and wherein encryption keys are derived from user credentials using zero-knowledge principles.
  5. Natural Language Voice Query Engine — An engine that parses natural language voice queries, performs vector similarity search across the document vault, retrieves matching documents, extracts relevant fields, and synthesizes voice responses containing the requested information, with support for contextual follow-up queries.
  6. Autonomous Call Management Module — A module that initiates outbound telephone calls via a telephony API, autonomously navigates automated phone system (IVR) menus using information stored in the vault, establishes conference connections including the user and the AI agent, maintains the AI as a persistent call participant with real-time document vault access, and transcribes calls in real-time with speaker identification.
  7. Post-Call Intelligence Module — A module that analyzes call transcriptions to extract action items, dates, monetary amounts, commitments, and follow-up requirements, and that automatically generates calendar events, reminders, document updates, and summary records stored in the vault.
  8. Application Interface Layer — A modular interface that enables domain-specific applications to extend the core engine with specialized workflows, domain knowledge, and compliance requirements without modification of the core modules.

1 System Architecture Overview

1.1 Hardware Platform

In preferred embodiments, the system is implemented as a software application executing on a mobile computing device, such as a smartphone or tablet computer running the iOS or Android operating system. The mobile device comprises at least: a multi-core processor with neural processing unit (NPU) or graphics processing unit (GPU) capable of running machine learning inference; a camera module capable of capturing high-resolution images of physical documents; a microphone array for audio input; a speaker or audio output interface; non-volatile storage sufficient to maintain the encrypted document vault; and wireless communication interfaces including cellular and Wi-Fi connectivity.

1.2 On-Device Processing Architecture

A defining characteristic of the present invention is that core functions—including wake word detection, speech-to-text conversion, optical character recognition, document encryption and storage, natural language query processing, and voice response synthesis—are performed entirely on the mobile device without requiring network connectivity. This on-device processing architecture ensures that: (a) sensitive user data, including spoken audio and document content, is not transmitted to remote servers; (b) the system remains functional during network outages; and (c) processing latency is minimized by eliminating network round-trip delays.

1.3 Cloud Connectivity

The system utilizes network connectivity selectively for functions that inherently require it:

  • Telephone Communications: Outbound and inbound telephone calls are established through a telephony API service (e.g., Twilio, Retell.ai, or equivalent) that bridges between Voice over Internet Protocol (VoIP) and the public switched telephone network (PSTN).
  • Calendar Synchronization: Calendar events generated by the post-call intelligence module are synchronized with the user's existing calendar service (e.g., Google Calendar, Apple Calendar, Microsoft Outlook) through standard calendar APIs.
  • Optional Encrypted Backup: Users may optionally enable end-to-end encrypted cloud backup of the document vault, wherein encryption is performed on-device before transmission and decryption keys are never transmitted to or stored on any server.

1.4 Operating Modes

The system operates in three primary modes, each of which may be initiated by voice command following wake word detection:

  • Document Capture Mode: The user captures and categorizes physical documents using the camera and voice-guided wizard.
  • Voice Query Mode: The user speaks natural language questions and receives synthesized voice responses from the document vault.
  • Call Participation Mode: The system initiates and participates in telephone calls with real-time document vault access, IVR navigation, and post-call intelligence extraction.

1.5 Data Flow Architecture

In operation, data flows through the system as follows. Audio input from the microphone is continuously monitored by the wake word detection module, which operates as a lightweight, always-on process. Upon wake word detection, the audio stream is routed to the on-device speech-to-text engine, which converts spoken audio to text. The resulting text is passed to an intent classification module that determines the user's intent and routes the request to the appropriate processing pipeline: document capture, voice query, or call initiation. Each pipeline operates on the encrypted document vault as its primary data store, reading from and writing to the vault as required. Voice responses are generated by the text-to-speech synthesis module and delivered through the device speaker or connected audio output.

2 On-Device Wake Word Detection

2.1 Architecture

The wake word detection module comprises a lightweight neural network model optimized for continuous execution on the mobile device's processor or dedicated neural processing unit. In preferred embodiments, the module employs a keyword-spotting architecture such as that provided by Picovoice Porcupine, Snowboy, or an equivalent on-device wake word engine. The model is trained to detect one or more predefined wake word phrases with high accuracy and low false-positive rates.

2.2 Power Efficiency

The wake word detection module is designed to operate continuously without significant impact on device battery life. In preferred embodiments, the module consumes less than approximately 5% of total device battery capacity over a 24-hour period when operating continuously. This efficiency is achieved through: (a) use of a small neural network model (typically less than 2 MB in size) that requires minimal computational resources; (b) operation at a reduced audio sampling rate sufficient for wake word detection but lower than that required for full speech-to-text processing; and (c) utilization of hardware-accelerated audio processing features available on modern mobile processors.

2.3 Privacy Characteristics

The wake word detection module processes all audio on-device and does not transmit any audio data, metadata, or detection events to any remote server. Audio that does not match the wake word pattern is discarded immediately and is not stored, buffered, or logged. Only upon positive wake word detection is the audio stream routed to the speech-to-text engine for further processing, and even then, processing occurs entirely on-device.

2.4 Customization

In preferred embodiments, users may configure one or more custom wake word phrases. The system supports both pre-trained wake word models (e.g., "Hey [Application Name]") and user-trained custom wake words created through a brief voice enrollment process. Multiple wake word variants may be active simultaneously, enabling users to select the phrase that is most natural and comfortable for their usage patterns.

2.5 Activation Pipeline

Upon positive wake word detection, the module performs the following sequence: (a) emits a configurable audio or haptic acknowledgment to confirm activation; (b) transitions the speech-to-text engine from standby to active processing mode; (c) begins routing the audio stream to the speech-to-text engine; and (d) initiates a configurable timeout period (e.g., 10 seconds of silence) after which the system returns to wake-word-only listening mode.

3 On-Device Speech-to-Text Processing

3.1 Inference Engine

The speech-to-text module comprises an on-device inference engine that converts spoken audio to text without transmitting audio data to any remote server. In preferred embodiments, the engine employs a transformer-based speech recognition model, such as those derived from the OpenAI Whisper architecture (e.g., whisper.cpp compiled for mobile platforms, or whisper.rn for React Native applications), optimized for on-device execution through quantization, pruning, and hardware-specific compilation.

3.2 Processing Modes

The speech-to-text engine supports two processing modes:

  • Real-Time Streaming Mode: Audio is processed in overlapping chunks (e.g., 30-second windows with 5-second overlap) to provide near-real-time transcription with partial result updates. This mode is used during voice queries and live call transcription.
  • Batch Processing Mode: Complete audio recordings are processed in a single pass for maximum accuracy. This mode is used for post-call transcription refinement and audio note processing.

3.3 Voice Activity Detection

The module incorporates a voice activity detection (VAD) subsystem that distinguishes speech from background noise and silence. The VAD subsystem enables efficient processing by: (a) activating the full inference engine only when speech is detected; (b) segmenting continuous speech into logical utterances based on pause detection; (c) filtering environmental noise to improve recognition accuracy; and (d) detecting end-of-utterance to trigger downstream intent classification.

3.4 Language Support

The speech-to-text engine supports multiple languages through downloadable language model packages. Users may install one or more language models on their device, and the system can be configured to automatically detect the spoken language or to operate in a fixed-language mode for improved accuracy. In preferred embodiments, the system supports at least English, Spanish, French, German, Mandarin Chinese, Japanese, Korean, Portuguese, and Arabic.

3.5 Intent Classification

The output of the speech-to-text engine is passed to an intent classification module that determines the type of user request. The intent classifier categorizes each utterance into one of the following primary intent categories: (a) document query (the user is asking a question that should be answered from the vault); (b) document capture (the user wishes to scan and store a document); (c) call initiation (the user wishes to place a telephone call); (d) system command (the user is adjusting settings, creating categories, or performing administrative tasks); or (e) conversational (the user is engaging in general conversation with the AI assistant). The classified intent determines which processing pipeline receives the utterance for further handling.

4 On-Device Optical Character Recognition with Voice-Guided Categorization

4.1 Document Capture Process

The document capture module enables users to digitize physical documents through the mobile device camera with automated text extraction and voice-guided categorization. The capture process proceeds as follows:

  1. The user activates document capture mode by voice command (e.g., "Scan a document") or by manual interface interaction.
  2. The device camera activates with real-time edge detection that identifies document boundaries within the camera viewfinder.
  3. Upon detection of a stable document position, the system captures a high-resolution image and applies perspective correction, contrast enhancement, and de-skewing to produce a normalized document image.
  4. The normalized image is processed by the on-device OCR engine.

4.2 OCR Engine

The OCR engine operates entirely on-device using a machine learning-based text recognition framework (e.g., Google ML Kit, Apple Vision framework, or Tesseract compiled for mobile). The engine extracts text from the captured document image with support for: printed text in multiple languages; handwritten text (with reduced accuracy); tabular data and structured layouts; and mixed text and graphical content. The extracted text is stored as a searchable text layer associated with the original document image.

4.3 Automatic Field Extraction

Following OCR text extraction, the system applies named entity recognition (NER) and pattern matching to automatically identify and extract structured fields from the document content, including but not limited to:

  • Dates: Expiration dates, effective dates, appointment dates, due dates.
  • Monetary Amounts: Deductibles, premiums, balances, payment amounts, copays.
  • Personal Names: Account holders, providers, beneficiaries, dependents.
  • Identification Numbers: Policy numbers, account numbers, member IDs, Social Security numbers (stored with enhanced encryption).
  • Addresses: Physical addresses, mailing addresses, provider locations.
  • Contact Information: Phone numbers, fax numbers, email addresses, website URLs.

Extracted fields are stored as structured metadata associated with the document record, enabling both precise field-based queries (e.g., "What is my policy number?") and broader semantic queries (e.g., "Tell me about my health coverage").

4.4 Voice-Guided Categorization Wizard

Following text extraction and field identification, the system initiates a voice-guided categorization wizard that assists the user in classifying and organizing the captured document. The wizard operates as follows:

  1. Content-Aware Category Suggestion: Based on the extracted text content and identified fields, the system suggests relevant categories. For example, a document containing terms such as "premium," "deductible," and "coverage" may prompt: "This looks like an insurance document. Would you like to file it under Health Insurance?"
  2. Hierarchical Navigation: If the user requests a different category or if automatic detection is uncertain, the wizard presents the top-level categories (rooms) and allows the user to navigate deeper into the hierarchy through voice responses. For example: "Is this for Personal, Work, Family, Health, or Pets?" followed by "Within Health, is this for Insurance, Medical Records, or Prescriptions?"
  3. Tag Assignment: The wizard offers the option to assign one or more tags for additional cross-referencing. Tags may be suggested based on document content or specified by the user in natural language.
  4. Confirmation: The wizard provides a verbal summary of the categorization and requests confirmation before finalizing storage.

4.5 Supported Document Types

The system is designed to capture and process a wide variety of document types encountered in personal and professional life, including but not limited to: insurance cards and policy documents; medical records, lab results, and prescriptions; residential leases, mortgage documents, and property records; utility bills, bank statements, and financial documents; receipts and warranties; government-issued identification documents; tax forms and filings; veterinary records and pet documentation; vehicle titles, registrations, and maintenance records; and educational transcripts and certifications.

5 Encrypted Hierarchical Document Vault

5.1 Local-First Architecture

The document vault is implemented as a local-first encrypted database stored on the mobile device's non-volatile storage. All document data—including raw images, extracted text, structured fields, vector embeddings, and metadata—is encrypted at rest using AES-256 encryption (or equivalent) before being written to storage. The local-first architecture ensures that: (a) no unencrypted document data is transmitted to any server under any circumstances during normal operation; (b) the vault is fully accessible without network connectivity; and (c) the user retains complete physical control over their data by virtue of its storage on their personal device.

5.2 Zero-Knowledge Encryption

The vault employs a zero-knowledge encryption scheme in which encryption keys are derived from user-provided credentials (e.g., a passphrase, biometric authentication, or a combination thereof) using a key derivation function (e.g., Argon2id, PBKDF2, or scrypt). Critically, the derived encryption keys are never transmitted to, stored on, or accessible by any remote server. The system operator has no ability to decrypt vault contents, even if compelled, because the operator never possesses the encryption keys. This zero-knowledge design provides strong privacy guarantees that are particularly important for sensitive personal documents such as medical records, financial information, and identity credentials.

5.3 Hierarchical Category Structure

Documents within the vault are organized in a user-defined hierarchical category structure referred to as "rooms." Each room represents a top-level organizational domain, and rooms may contain sub-categories to any desired depth. The default room structure may include, for example:

  • Personal: Identity documents, personal correspondence, personal records.
  • Work: Employment documents, professional certifications, business records.
  • Family: Family member documents, shared household records, school records.
  • Health: Insurance, medical records, prescriptions, lab results, provider contacts.
  • Pets: Veterinary records, vaccination records, insurance, registration.
  • Financial: Bank statements, tax documents, investment records, bills.
  • Home: Lease/mortgage, utilities, maintenance records, warranties.
  • Vehicle: Title, registration, insurance, maintenance, loan documents.

Users may create, rename, reorganize, and delete rooms and sub-categories at any time through voice commands or manual interface interaction. The hierarchical structure is stored as metadata within the encrypted vault.

5.4 Document Record Structure

Each document stored in the vault comprises a document record containing the following elements:

  • Raw Image: The original captured image of the physical document, stored in its original resolution.
  • Extracted Text: The full OCR-extracted text content of the document.
  • Structured Fields: Key-value pairs of identified fields (e.g., "Policy Number: ABC123456", "Expiration Date: 2027-03-15").
  • Vector Embedding: A high-dimensional vector representation of the document content generated by an on-device embedding model, enabling semantic similarity search.
  • Category Path: The hierarchical room and sub-category assignment (e.g., "Health > Insurance > Auto").
  • Tags: Zero or more user-assigned or automatically suggested tags for cross-referencing.
  • Capture Metadata: Timestamp, device information, and capture quality indicators.
  • Revision History: A log of modifications to the document record, including re-categorizations and field corrections.

5.5 Vector-Indexed Search

The vault maintains a vector index over all stored document embeddings, enabling semantic similarity search in response to natural language queries. The vector index is implemented using an on-device approximate nearest neighbor (ANN) algorithm (e.g., HNSW, IVF, or equivalent) that provides sub-second query performance even for vaults containing thousands of documents. The vector index is updated incrementally as documents are added, modified, or deleted. Combined with traditional keyword search over extracted text and structured fields, the vector index enables the system to answer both precise queries ("What is my Geico policy number?") and semantic queries ("What documents do I have about car coverage?") with high relevance.

5.6 Cross-Category Intelligence

The natural language query engine is capable of performing cross-category searches that span multiple rooms and sub-categories. For example, the query "What insurance do I have for my dog?" may retrieve documents from both the "Pets" room (veterinary records mentioning insurance) and the "Financial" room (pet insurance policy documents). Cross-category intelligence is enabled by: (a) vector similarity search that operates across the entire vault regardless of category boundaries; (b) tag-based correlation that identifies documents sharing common tags across categories; and (c) entity linking that connects documents mentioning the same entities (e.g., a pet's name, a provider's name, a policy number) across categories.

5.7 Optional Encrypted Cloud Backup

Users may optionally enable cloud backup of the encrypted vault. When enabled, the encrypted vault contents are transmitted to a cloud storage service (e.g., iCloud, Google Drive, or a dedicated backup service) in their encrypted form. Because encryption is performed on-device before transmission, the cloud storage provider receives only encrypted data and cannot access vault contents. Backup and restoration are initiated by the user through voice command or manual interface interaction. The system supports incremental backup to minimize data transfer requirements.

6 Natural Language Voice Query Engine

6.1 Query Processing Pipeline

When the intent classification module determines that a user utterance constitutes a document query, the utterance is processed through the following pipeline:

  1. Query Understanding: The natural language text is analyzed to determine the query type (factual lookup, document retrieval, comparison, enumeration, or action request), the target entity or topic, and any constraints (date ranges, categories, specific document types).
  2. Search Execution: The query engine executes a hybrid search combining: (a) vector similarity search using an embedding of the query text against the vault's vector index; (b) keyword search against extracted text fields; and (c) structured field lookup for queries targeting specific known fields (e.g., "policy number," "expiration date"). Search results are ranked by a combined relevance score.
  3. Document Retrieval: The top-ranked matching documents are retrieved from the encrypted vault and decrypted in-memory for processing.
  4. Field Extraction: Based on the query type, the engine extracts the specific information requested from the retrieved documents. For factual lookups, this may be a single field value; for broader queries, this may be a synthesis of information from multiple documents.
  5. Response Generation: The extracted information is formatted into a natural language response suitable for voice synthesis.
  6. Voice Synthesis: The response text is converted to speech using an on-device text-to-speech (TTS) engine and delivered through the device speaker or connected audio output.

6.2 Contextual Follow-Up Queries

The query engine maintains a conversation context buffer that enables follow-up queries to reference the context of prior exchanges. For example:

  • User: "When does my lease expire?" → System retrieves lease, responds with expiration date.
  • User: "What's the landlord's phone number?" → System understands "the landlord" refers to the lease just retrieved, extracts and speaks the phone number.
  • User: "Call them." → System understands "them" refers to the landlord, initiates a call to the extracted phone number, and pre-loads the lease document for reference during the call.

The context buffer retains the most recent query-response pairs and their associated document references for a configurable duration or number of turns.

6.3 Example Queries and Responses

The following examples illustrate the range of queries the system is designed to handle:

  • "When does my lease expire?" → Retrieves lease document → "Your lease expires on August 31, 2027."
  • "What's my insurance deductible?" → Retrieves insurance card → "Your annual deductible is $1,500 for in-network providers and $3,000 for out-of-network."
  • "Do I have any documents expiring this month?" → Searches all documents with expiration dates → "You have two documents expiring this month: your auto insurance policy on April 15th and your driver's license on April 28th."
  • "What shots does my dog need?" → Retrieves veterinary records → "Based on your vet records, Bella is due for her rabies booster and bordetella vaccination. Her last visit was November 12th."
  • "How much did I pay for car repairs last year?" → Retrieves automotive receipts → "I found four repair receipts from 2025 totaling $2,847.50."

6.4 Voice Response Synthesis

The text-to-speech synthesis module converts response text to spoken audio using an on-device neural TTS engine. In preferred embodiments, the TTS engine supports: multiple voice options (varying in gender, accent, and speaking rate); configurable speaking rate and volume; intelligent handling of numbers, dates, monetary amounts, and alphanumeric identifiers (e.g., spelling out policy numbers letter-by-letter); and pause insertion for natural phrasing of complex responses.

7 Autonomous Call Participation Module

This section describes the most novel component of the present invention.

7a. Call Initiation

7a.1 Voice-Commanded Call Initiation

The autonomous call participation module enables users to initiate telephone calls through voice commands. Upon receiving a call initiation intent (e.g., "Call Geico," "Call Dr. Smith's office," "Call my landlord"), the system performs the following preparatory steps:

  1. Contact Resolution: The system queries the document vault and the device's native contact list to resolve the specified entity to a telephone number. Resolution may involve: matching the entity name against document metadata (e.g., finding a phone number on a stored insurance card for "Geico"); searching extracted text fields for phone numbers associated with the entity; or looking up the entity in the device's native contact database.
  2. Document Pre-Loading: The system identifies and pre-loads documents likely to be relevant to the upcoming call. For example, a call to "Geico" triggers pre-loading of: the Geico insurance policy document, the insurance ID card, any recent correspondence from Geico, and any prior call summaries with Geico. Pre-loaded documents are decrypted in-memory and indexed for rapid access during the call.
  3. User Briefing: Before initiating the call, the system provides the user with a brief summary: "I'll call Geico at (800) 861-8380. I have your policy number, your claim from January, and your most recent bill ready. Shall I proceed?"
  4. Call Establishment: Upon user confirmation, the system initiates the outbound call through a telephony API (e.g., Twilio Programmable Voice, Retell.ai, or equivalent) that bridges between VoIP and the PSTN. The AI agent is connected to the call as the initial participant.

7a.2 Telephony Integration

The call is established through a telephony API service that provides: outbound PSTN calling capability; real-time audio streaming for the AI agent's participation; conference call management (adding and removing participants); call recording and real-time transcription support; and DTMF tone generation for IVR menu navigation. The telephony integration is abstracted behind a provider-agnostic interface, enabling substitution of telephony providers without modification to the core application logic.

7b. IVR Navigation

7b.1 Automated Menu Navigation

Upon call connection, the AI agent autonomously navigates automated phone system (Interactive Voice Response, or IVR) menus on behalf of the user. The IVR navigation module operates as follows:

  1. Menu Detection: The agent's real-time speech recognition identifies IVR menu prompts and determines the available options (e.g., "Press 1 for claims, press 2 for billing, press 3 for new policies").
  2. Intent Mapping: Based on the user's stated reason for calling (if provided) or inferred purpose (based on pre-loaded documents), the agent selects the most appropriate menu option.
  3. DTMF Response: The agent generates the appropriate DTMF tone(s) to navigate the menu. For speech-based IVR systems, the agent provides spoken responses.
  4. Information Provision: When IVR prompts request personal information (e.g., "Please enter your policy number," "Please say your date of birth"), the agent retrieves the requested information from the pre-loaded documents and provides it via DTMF tones or speech as appropriate.
  5. Hold Detection: When placed on hold, the agent detects hold music or silence patterns and enters a monitoring state that consumes minimal processing resources while waiting for a human representative to answer.

7b.2 User Notification During Hold

While the AI agent waits on hold, the user is free to use their device for other purposes. The system provides: an estimated wait time (if announced by the IVR system); periodic status notifications (e.g., "Still on hold with Geico. Estimated wait: 12 minutes."); and an immediate notification when a human representative answers (e.g., "A Geico representative has answered. Would you like to join the call?").

7c. Persistent AI Participation

7c.1 Conference Mode

When a human representative answers the call, the system supports two operating modes:

  • Full AI Mode: The AI agent conducts the entire conversation with the human representative on behalf of the user, following pre-established instructions and utilizing information from the document vault. The user receives a real-time transcript and can interject at any time.
  • Conference Mode (Preferred): The user is conferenced into the call as an active participant. The AI agent remains connected as a persistent third participant with the following capabilities:
    • Real-time document vault access to answer questions from any participant.
    • Automatic provision of requested information (e.g., when the representative asks "What's your policy number?", the AI can immediately provide it from the vault).
    • Real-time transcription displayed on the user's device screen.
    • Whisper mode: the AI can provide information to the user through text display or private audio channel without the call counterpart hearing.

7c.2 Warm-Transfer Mode

In a preferred embodiment, the system supports a warm-transfer mode in which: (a) the AI agent initiates the call and handles all initial interactions including IVR navigation, hold waiting, and initial representative greeting; (b) the AI provides the representative with basic account verification information; (c) the AI briefs the user on what has been discussed so far; and (d) the user is then conferenced in with full context, avoiding the need to repeat information. The AI remains as a persistent passive participant that responds when directly addressed or when document information is requested.

7c.3 Document-Aware Responses

Throughout the call, the AI agent maintains active awareness of the conversation context and the pre-loaded document set. When a question is asked that can be answered from the vault—whether by the call counterpart or by the user themselves—the AI can provide the answer. Examples include:

  • Representative: "What's your policy number?" → AI: "The policy number is GEI-4827193." (read from insurance card in vault)
  • Representative: "When was your last claim?" → AI: "The most recent claim was filed on January 15, 2026, for incident reference CLM-88421." (read from correspondence in vault)
  • User: "What was our deductible again?" → AI whispers to user: "Your deductible is $500 for comprehensive and $1,000 for collision."

7d. Live Transcription

7d.1 Real-Time Transcription

The entire call is transcribed in real-time using the system's speech-to-text engine. The transcription module provides:

  • Speaker Diarization: The system identifies and labels each speaker in the transcript (user, AI agent, call counterpart), enabling clear attribution of statements.
  • Real-Time Display: The transcript is displayed on the user's device screen in real-time, with speaker labels and timestamps.
  • Keyword Highlighting: Important terms such as monetary amounts, dates, names, and action-related language are highlighted in the real-time display.
  • Consent Notification: At the beginning of each call, the system provides an appropriate disclosure that the call may be recorded and transcribed, in compliance with applicable federal and state recording consent laws.

7d.2 Transcript Storage

Upon call completion, the full transcript is encrypted and stored in the document vault under the relevant category, associated with: the call date, time, and duration; the participating parties; the call outcome classification; and a generated summary (see Section 7e).

7e. Post-Call Intelligence

7e.1 Transcript Analysis

Upon call completion, the post-call intelligence module analyzes the full call transcript to extract actionable information, including:

  • Action Items: Tasks that the user or any party committed to during the call (e.g., "I'll send you that form by Friday," "You need to submit the documentation within 30 days").
  • Dates and Deadlines: Specific dates, deadlines, and time-sensitive commitments mentioned during the call (e.g., "Your appointment is Tuesday at 2 PM," "The appeal deadline is April 30th").
  • Monetary Amounts: Financial figures discussed during the call (e.g., "Your balance is $847.23," "The copay will be $35").
  • Commitments and Promises: Statements of intent or commitment by any party (e.g., "We'll process your refund within 5-7 business days," "I'll follow up with the supervisor").
  • Reference Numbers: Case numbers, confirmation numbers, reference IDs, and tracking numbers provided during the call.
  • Contact Information: Names, direct phone numbers, and extension numbers of representatives spoken with.

7e.2 Automated Action Generation

Based on the extracted information, the post-call intelligence module automatically generates:

  • Calendar Events: Appointments, deadlines, and follow-up dates are converted to calendar events and synchronized with the user's calendar service. Events include relevant context (e.g., "Follow-up call with Geico re: claim CLM-88421, reference: Sarah ext. 4521").
  • Reminders: Time-sensitive action items generate reminders at appropriate intervals (e.g., a reminder 3 days before a deadline, a reminder to follow up if a promised callback has not occurred).
  • Document Updates: New information obtained during the call (e.g., updated balances, new reference numbers, revised dates) is flagged for integration into existing vault documents or stored as supplementary records.
  • Call Summary: A structured summary of the call is generated, including: participants, duration, key topics discussed, decisions made, action items, and next steps. The summary is stored in the vault and optionally delivered to the user via notification.

7e.3 Example Post-Call Workflow

To illustrate the complete post-call intelligence workflow, consider the following example. A user calls their health insurance provider to dispute a claim. During the call, the representative states: "Your appeal has been received. The review committee meets on April 15th. You should receive a determination letter within 10 business days after that. If you need to add any documentation, please submit it by April 10th. Your case number is APL-29471. I'm Sarah, and my direct extension is 4521."

The post-call intelligence module generates the following automated actions:

  • Calendar event: "Insurance Appeal Review Committee Meeting" on April 15th.
  • Reminder: "Submit additional documentation for appeal APL-29471" on April 8th (2 days before deadline).
  • Reminder: "Expected determination letter for appeal APL-29471" on April 29th (10 business days after April 15th).
  • Document update: New reference number APL-29471 added to health insurance records.
  • Contact update: "Sarah, ext. 4521" stored as insurance provider contact.
  • Call summary stored in vault under Health > Insurance with full transcript.

8 Multi-Application Deployment Platform

8.1 Platform Architecture

The system described in the preceding sections is designed as a modular, application-agnostic platform that powers multiple domain-specific applications through a common core engine. The platform architecture comprises two layers:

  • Core Engine Layer: The shared infrastructure including the wake word detection module, speech-to-text engine, OCR module, encrypted document vault, natural language query engine, voice response synthesizer, autonomous call management module, and post-call intelligence module. The core engine is domain-agnostic and provides general-purpose voice, document, and communication capabilities.
  • Application Interface Layer: A well-defined API and extension mechanism that enables domain-specific applications to: register specialized document types and field extractors; add domain-specific vocabulary and intent categories to the speech processing pipeline; implement specialized workflows triggered by voice commands or post-call events; overlay domain-specific compliance requirements (e.g., HIPAA for healthcare, PCI-DSS for financial); customize the voice interaction patterns and response formats; and integrate with domain-specific external services and APIs.

8.2 Healthcare Application Module

In a first exemplary application, the platform powers a healthcare coordination application that extends the core engine with:

  • Medical Document Types: Specialized extractors for medical records, lab results, prescription documents, EOB (Explanation of Benefits) statements, and provider directories.
  • Medication Management: Extraction and tracking of medication names, dosages, frequencies, and refill dates from prescription documents, with automated reminders.
  • Appointment Coordination: Enhanced post-call intelligence that identifies appointment-related information and integrates with healthcare provider scheduling systems.
  • HIPAA Compliance: Additional encryption, access logging, and data handling controls that satisfy Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act requirements for protected health information (PHI).
  • Provider Network Awareness: Integration with insurance provider directories to support queries such as "Is Dr. Smith in my network?" and "Find me an in-network dermatologist near me."
  • Medical Terminology: Domain-specific vocabulary added to the speech recognition and natural language understanding models to improve accuracy for medical terms, drug names, and procedure codes.

8.3 Life Management Application Module

In a second exemplary application, the platform powers a personal life management application that extends the core engine with:

  • Multi-Room Organization: Enhanced hierarchical vault organization with pre-configured room templates for common life domains (home, auto, pets, family, finances).
  • Service Provider Coordination: Workflows for managing relationships with service providers (landlords, utilities, contractors, veterinarians) including automated call preparation with relevant documents.
  • Household Intelligence: Cross-category queries that synthesize information across multiple life domains (e.g., "What bills are due this week?" spanning utilities, insurance, and subscriptions).
  • Contextual Recommendations: Proactive suggestions based on document analysis (e.g., "Your auto insurance expires next month. Would you like me to get comparison quotes?" or "Your pet's vaccination is overdue. Shall I call the vet?").
  • Affiliate Service Integration: Optional integration with vetted service providers that can be contacted or engaged through the AI call module, enabling a concierge-like experience for common life management tasks.

8.4 Extensibility for Additional Verticals

The platform's modular architecture is designed to support rapid deployment of additional domain-specific applications beyond the exemplary healthcare and life management modules. Potential vertical applications include but are not limited to: legal case management; small business operations; elder care coordination; education and credential management; travel and logistics; and real estate transaction management. Each vertical application leverages the same core engine while adding domain-specific knowledge, workflows, and integrations through the application interface layer.

8.2 Key Differentiators

8.2.1 No Existing System Combines On-Device Processing with Autonomous Call Participation

The most significant novel aspect of the present invention is the combination of on-device voice processing with autonomous telephone call participation that has real-time access to a persistent encrypted document vault. While individual components of the system (voice assistants, document storage, telephony APIs) exist in isolation, no known prior art combines these elements into an integrated system where an AI agent can: initiate calls using voice commands; navigate IVR systems using stored personal information; participate in live calls as a persistent agent with real-time document access; and extract actionable intelligence from completed calls. This combination creates an entirely new category of AI assistant functionality that fundamentally changes how users interact with service providers over the telephone.

8.2.2 Privacy-First Architecture

Unlike existing voice assistants that transmit substantially all voice data to cloud servers for processing, the present invention performs all core processing on-device. This privacy-first architecture is not merely a design preference but an enabling feature for the system's handling of sensitive personal documents including medical records, financial information, and identity credentials. The zero-knowledge encryption scheme ensures that neither the system operator nor any third party can access vault contents, a capability that existing cloud-dependent voice assistants cannot provide.

8.2.3 Voice-Guided Document Capture with Intelligent Categorization

No existing system provides voice-guided document capture with content-aware categorization suggestions. While OCR capabilities exist in isolation (e.g., Google Lens, Adobe Scan), and voice assistants exist in isolation, no known prior art combines on-device OCR with a voice interaction wizard that adapts its categorization suggestions based on detected content and stores documents in an encrypted hierarchical vault with vector-indexed search.

8.2.4 Platform Modularity for Domain-Specific Applications

The application interface layer that enables domain-specific applications to extend the core engine without modifying it represents a novel architectural approach to AI assistant development. Unlike proprietary voice assistant ecosystems (Siri, Alexa, Google) that provide limited extension mechanisms through skills or actions, the present invention provides a comprehensive application interface that enables deep integration of domain-specific knowledge, document types, workflows, compliance requirements, and external services.
